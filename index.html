This Week's Recipe: Sea Scallops with Caluiflower and Aspargus topped with a Red Pepper Sauce

Let's Get Started

Items You Need

Ingredients

  • 8-Sea Scallops
  • 3-Red Bell Peppers
  • 10-Asparagus
  • Head of Cauliflower (will trim into florets)
  • 1 1/2-Cups Heavy Cream
  • 3-Garlic Cloves
  • Salt and Pepper
  • Oil (Vegetable or Canola)
  • Parsely or Basil (as a garnish)

Equipment

  • 1-Pot
  • 1-Blender
  • 1-Fine Stainer
  • 3-Sauté Pans

Preparation

General Preparation

Begin by preheating the oven to 375 degrees. Cut the asparagus into smaller pieces, only use the heads (see photo below). Cut the culiflower into smaller pieces, about 10 pieces in total. Cut the smaller pieces in half so they create a flat surface. Roast the red peppers over an open flame until a fully blacken char is achieved. Place the red peppers into a ziploc bag, seal it, and leave for about 10-15 minutes.

Sauce Preparation

In a pot, add 1 teaspoon of oil and let the pot get hot. When hot, add the garlic to the pot. Sweat the garlic so there is no color on it. Add the heavy cream, salt, and pepper. Bring it to a boil, then simmer until reduced by 1/4. After the 10-15 minutes in the ziploc bag, remove the peppers and rub the char off with a towel. Remove the top, bottom, and inside ring of pepper as well as the seeds. Chop the peppers into smaller pieces and add into a blender. Add 1/8 cup of oil and blend until puréed. Add the heavy cream and belnd until thoroughly mixed. When the sauce has a smooth consistency, strain through a fine strainer into a bowl and set aside.

Directions

Scallops

In a sauté pan, add oil and get the pan very hot. Season the scallops with salt and pepper (on a cutting board) and add to the pan when it's very hot. Achieve a golden brown color on both sides. Finish in the oven for 3-4 minutes.

Vegetables

In a sauté pan, add oil and get the pan very hot. Add the asparagus, season with salt and pepper. Sauté until brown. Finish in the oven for 3-4 minutes. In a sauté pan, add oil and get the pan very hot. Add the cauliflower, season with salt and pepper. Wait to achieve a golden brown color on the flat surface. When achieved, give them a few tosses in the pan and finish in the oven for 3-4 minutes.

Sauce

In a cold sauté pan, add the red pepper sauce that was strained and bring to a boil. The simmer and reduce until the consistency is nappant. Tip: Nappant means that the sauce is thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.

Plating

When the sauce is ready, take a spoon and carefully spoon onto plate, creating a design. Then add the asparagus and cauliflower. Followed by the scallops (4 per plate). Finish with a little more red pepper sauce on top of the scallops. Sprinkle fresh parley on top for a garnish.
